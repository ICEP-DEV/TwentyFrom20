About Us


Tshwane University of Technology (TUT) is popularly known as "The People’s University" due to its commitment to providing accessible, quality education across South Africa. Established in 2004 through the merger of three technikons, TUT has grown into the largest contact university in the country. It operates a multi-campus system with a significant presence in five provinces. TUT is a vibrant and diverse institution dedicated to advancing knowledge and fostering innovation, with a thriving community of over 60,000 students.

The university has seven faculties, offering an extensive range of qualifications that are fully accredited by relevant educational authorities. In addition to its academic offerings, TUT is deeply involved in research and community engagement, playing a crucial role in addressing societal challenges and contributing to sustainable development. The university’s strategic initiatives aim to create a positive impact on local communities while preparing students to excel in a rapidly evolving global landscape.

#Twentyfrom20 Campaign


The aim of the #Twentyfrom20 campaign is to raise R1.2 million by partnering with 60 companies, each donating R20,000, to support three key projects that will empower future leaders and innovators. Running from the 1st of September to the 29th of November, this campaign seeks to build long-term partnerships with companies committed to advancing education and community development. Through your generous contributions, TUT can continue to offer transformative educational opportunities, expand its research initiatives, and make a lasting difference in the lives of students and the communities in which the university operates.
